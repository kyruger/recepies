Lahmacun

for the dough;

  • 1 cup of warm milk
  • 1 cup of warm water
  • hafl cup of oil
  • 1 desertspoon of sugar
  • 2 desertspoon of salt
  • 1 packet of instant or fresh yeast
  • 700g of flour

for souce;

  • 400 g medium-fat beef minced meat
  • 3 large charleston peppers
  • 2 onion
  • 2 handful of parsley
  • 1 small cup of oil
  • 2 peeled and grated tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoon of pepper paste
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic
  • 2 teaspoon of sumac
  • 1 teaspoon of vegeta
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 deserspoon of sat

How To Make

  1. For the dough, we take warm water, milk and sugar into the kneading bowl.

  2. Then we add the yeast and let it stand for 5-10 minutes for the yeast to dissolve.

  3. With the addition of oil, salt and slow flour, we knead the dough so that it does not become soft and does not stick to the hand.

  4. We cover it and leave it to ferment for 1 hour.

  5. While the dough is leavening, we are preparing the minced meat mortar of lahmacun in a separate container.

  6. On top of our minced meat, we put onion, pepper and garlic, which we have chopped very finely.

  7. We grate the tomato in a separate container and add 2 tablespoons of our pepper paste to it and mix it very well.

  8. And we pour this sauce over our minced meat.

  9. We add the oil, spices and salt last and knead very well.

  10. After the dough is fermented, we divide it into palm-sized meringues.

  11. We spread the flour on the bottom of the meringues and open them thinly.

  12. We spread the minced meat mortar about 2 spoons on it and bake in a preheated oven at 200 degrees for about 5-6 minutes.
